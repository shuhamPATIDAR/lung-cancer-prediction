What is lung cancer?

Lung cancer forms in the lung tissues, often in the cells lining the airways. There are two main types: small cell and non-small cell, with non-small cell being the more common.

Symptoms of lung cancer:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • A persistent cough that worsens
  • Coughing up blood
  • Breathing difficulties or wheezing
  • Hoarseness
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Swelling in the face or neck veins

Who is more likely to develop lung cancer?

Anyone can develop lung cancer, but these risk factors raise your chances:

  • Smoking: Responsible for about 90% of lung cancer cases in men and 80% in women. Quitting reduces risk but does not eliminate it entirely.
  • Secondhand smoke: Exposure to smoke from others can increase risk.
  • Family history: A genetic predisposition can increase your chances of developing lung cancer.
  • Workplace exposure: Exposure to substances like asbestos, arsenic, and chromium raises risk.
  • Radiation exposure: Radiation therapy, radon, or imaging tests like CT scans can contribute to the risk.
  • HIV infection: Higher risk, possibly due to higher smoking rates in those with HIV.
  • Air pollution: Living in areas with high pollution levels can also elevate lung cancer risk.

Purpose of the Lung Cancer Prediction Model

  • Risk Assessment: The model predicts an individual's risk of developing lung cancer based on personal and behavioral factors.
  • Early Awareness: It helps users become aware of their lung cancer risk, encouraging early detection and preventive measures.
  • Educational Tool: The model provides information on lung cancer risk factors, symptoms, and trends, raising awareness and educating users.
  • Support Decision-Making: By offering risk insights, it assists users in making informed health decisions, such as seeking medical advice or lifestyle changes.
  • Accessible Screening: It offers an easily accessible, online platform for preliminary lung cancer risk screening, promoting public health awareness.
